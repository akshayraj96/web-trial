RIC

An initative for the Students, by the Students of Sri Krishna Institutions

What does RIC do?

RIC creates awareness of the value of research field in engineering and helps students to develop their skills apart from academics. It motivates student to participate and involve in research works. And this would lead to a better understanding of engineering applications and sculpt the knowledge of the student into a perfect statue. The marketing team does the job of creating awareness to the students. They help the students to choose their desired domain in their field (course) and explain to the students about the various domains that are in their fields. This would lead to a clear understanding of students about why they are studying their courses and would choose a domain based on what do they want to become. Once this has been chose by a student RIC provides opportunity to develop their knowledge in their specific domain and allows student to do research works and innovative projects on particular domains.

How it works?

The students who are willing to develop their skills, knowledge and research project may approach any student members (preferably student coordinators of respective department who can provide more information about their field) and can register their names and their research work. The students who are to do the research projects would have to submit their abstract to the Expert Team who will do their assigned job. And the students who want to develop can enrol themselves to the Management Team who will do the necessary work to help the students. The Cell encourages the students to work on paper publications and patenting their ideas. For any patents that are to be filed, the patenting cost will be funded by the Cell.

What else?

The Cell also organizes various Workshops, Seminars, competitions and bootcamps on technologies which will improve the technical knowledge of students. Every month has various events conducted by the Cell which would be beneficial to the students of the institution.
